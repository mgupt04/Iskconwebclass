Our Founder & Teachings

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), commonly known as the Hare Krishna Movement, is a Hindu religious organization founded in 1966 in New York City. Its teachings are based on the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition, which emphasizes devotion to Krishna as the Supreme Deity. The organization was established by A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, who, at the age of 69, traveled from India to the United States to fulfill his spiritual master's instruction to share the teachings of Krishna with the Western world.
